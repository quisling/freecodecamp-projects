Linus Torvalds
The Father of Linux and Git

via wikipedia, CC BY-SA 3.0

Here is a short timeline of Linus' life:

  • 1969: Born in Helsinki, Finland, named after Linus Pauling, an American Nobel Prize-winning chemist.
  • 1988: Started studying at the University of Helsinki.
  • 1989: Briefly interrupted his education to fulfill his mandatory military service by participating in their officer training program.
  • 1994: Released version 1.0 of Linux.
  • 1996: Graduated with a master's degree in Computer Science. Announces the same year that there will be a mascot for linux, a penguin.
  • 1997: Began working at Transmeta.
  • 2003: Moved on from Transmetra and started employment with the Open Source Development Labs.
  • 2005: Discontented with the restrictions of modern version control options, begins work on git. Version 1.0 is released later the same year.
  • 2007: His employer OSDL merged with the Free Standards Group to form the Linux Foundation.
  • 2010: Became a US Citizen.
  • 2014: Recipient of the IEEE Computer Society's Computer Pioneer Award.
